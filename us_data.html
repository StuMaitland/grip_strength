What is this?
Grip strength is associated with morbidity and mortality in older people. It is easy to measure and replicate. The normative values for grip strength vary by sex and age.

Less normative data is available for the US population than in Europe. This tool uses normative mean and standard deviation for age groups of five years separation. This allows an estimate of z-score We would encourage you to instead use the British version of this tool which is more fine-grained to 0.5 year age groups, and has a better representation of the dataset, using the LMS method to capture the shape of the normative data instead of assuming normal distribution as this tool does.

In the interests of data protection, all processing is performed client side- no information is transmitted, stored or tracked while you are using this.

Data Source: Reference values for adult grip strength measured with a Jamar dynamometer: a descriptive meta-analysis Bohannon et al. Physiotherapy Volume 92, Issue 1, March 2006, Pages 11-15 https://doi.org/10.1016/j.physio.2005.05.003
